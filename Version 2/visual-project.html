Overview

ActiveTextbook (ATB) is a desktop and mobile reading platform developed by Evident Point Software. In Fall 2017, a new feature called offline reading was being introduced to support book access without internet.

Download Process

For this feature, I was tasked with designing the assets needed to represent the different stages of the book downloading process. These icons were to be implemented into our Reader's toolbar where other functionalities reside and will be used to initiate the download feature.

Visual Style

I worked closely with our lead project developer to design all the icons we needed. One of the most challenging aspects of ideation was finding the right balance between creating something unique for the company but also conventional for the user.

Mockups

After initial sketches, I moved on to higher fidelity variations of the styles I thought would best fit into our current UI. Taking into account ATB's colour scheme and existing assets, I designed the icons to be consistent with the Reader for seamless integration.

I then shared my proposed styles with fellow coworkers to get fresh perspectives and evaluate which designs were most effective. Feedback sessions with the project developers also got us talking about the feasibility of implementing certain designs.

Final Icons

The final styles we chose were based on how well they communicated the stage they represented and how consistent they were with other icons in the UI. I added these designs to our sprite sheet before integrating the initial icons in our toolbar using CSS. The next step of the project was for our developers to implement the remaining icon states, which appear through server-side activity during the book download process.
